Liverpool

History

This is a website about Liverpool's History and Legends.

Liverpool football club was founded in 1892 by John Houlding after a dispute over which stadium ciy rivals Everton wanted to play in. Everton had to move to Goodison Park, while Liverpool played in Anfield. It was originally named "Everton F.C. and Athletic Grounds Ltd" but as this was too similar, the FA did not sanction it and the name was changed to Liverpool F.C, matching the city.

The team saw some early success but collapsed in the mid 1950s with relegation to the second division. In the 1959-1960 saeson, Bill Shankly was appointed manager. He revolutionized the club's tactics and philosophy, upgrading facilities and making a boot room into a tactical room.

After Bill Shankly, his assistant Bob Paisley took over. Shankly had instilled a winning culture back into the club, and Paisley built off of it. Over his 9 seasons of managing, he led the club to 20 trophies, including three European Cups, a UEFA Cup, six League titles and three consecutive League Cups; the only domestic trophy he did not win was the FA Cup.

After Paisley, the club won a couple more titles under Joe Fagan but he was forced to resign by the Heysel Stadium Disaster in 1985, during the 1985 European Cup Final. Liverpool fans broke a barrier in the stands, charging the Juventus fans and killing dozens. Liverpool were banned for 6 years and other English Clubs for 5 years. Fourteen Liverpool fans were convicted for involuntary manslaughter.

Kenny Dalglish became Player-Manager, but again disaster struck. 97 Liverpool Fans were crushed and killed during a crowd crush in a standing stadium with perimeter fencing. The FA made new guidelines for stadiums in the top division due to this.

The Hillsborough Disaster as it was called, had lasting effects on the club. They won 2 Champions League Titles among other domestic trophies but didn't win the league title for 30 years until Jurgen Klopp brought them to victory in 2019-20. Currently, the manager is Arne Slot.

Liverpool Legends

  • Ian Rush; 346 goals, 110 assists, 660 games. Led team to first treble in 1983-84 season
  • Steven Gerrard; 186 goals, 155 assists, 710 games. Led team to the Istanbul 'Miracle' 2005 comeback from 3-0 down, to win the Champions League Final.
  • Mohammed Salah; 224 goals, 100 assists, 366 games. Led team to Champions League 2019, Premier League 2020. Broke Premier League scoring record in Debut Season with 32 goals. 3x Premier League Top Scorer, 2x POTY.
